Guide

Cross-stitch & counted-craft charts from a photo

Counted crafts work from a chart: a grid of squares where each square is one stitch, bead, or pixel. GridGen overlays a precise, labeled counting grid on any photo or design and exports a print-ready PDF you can follow off-screen — so you can count rows and columns without losing your place.

A counting grid from a photo

Whether you stitch, bead, or place fuse beads, the work is the same underneath: you read a chart square by square and reproduce each one. A clear grid with labeled rows and columns is what makes that countable. GridGen turns your own photo or pattern image into exactly that grid — every cell the same size, every row and column numbered so you always know where you are.

A counting grid, not a pattern generator. GridGen overlays the grid and labels so you can count. It does not reduce your photo to a palette of thread colors or assign symbols to squares — you choose the colors as you work.

Step by step with GridGen

  1. Upload your photo or design. Drag it into GridGen, or click to select. It is processed in your browser and never uploaded.
  2. Choose By size. Set a fixed cell size in pixels so each square maps to one stitch, bead, or pixel — the same cell size across the whole image.
  3. Turn on labels. Numeric (1,1) labels number the rows and columns so you can count and keep your place; chess (A1) labels work too.
  4. Set line color and opacity. Pick a line that stands out against your image so the grid stays easy to read.
  5. Export the PDF. Download an A4 chart at full image resolution and follow it square by square.

If you are scaling a drawing by hand rather than counting stitches, the grid drawing method page covers that workflow.

Which crafts this suits

  • Cross-stitch & needlepoint — one cell per stitch, with counted rows and columns to track your progress on Aida or canvas.
  • Pixel art — one cell per pixel for drawing or planning sprites on paper.
  • Diamond painting — a counting grid to place drills row by row.
  • Beadwork & perler / fuse beads — the same photo-to-chart counting grid for bead placement.
  • Latch hook & rug making — coarse squares for blocking out areas of color.

What GridGen does and doesn't do

  • ✅ Overlays an even counting grid with labeled rows and columns on your image.
  • ✅ Exports a print-ready A4 PDF at your image's full resolution.
  • ✅ Runs entirely in your browser — your photo is never uploaded.
  • ❌ Does not reduce your photo to a limited thread or bead palette.
  • ❌ Does not assign symbols or DMC/Anchor color codes to squares.

If you need a fully symbol-coded pattern with a color key, a dedicated pattern converter is the right tool. GridGen is the fast, private way to get a clean, countable grid over an image you already have.

FAQ

Can I turn a photo into a cross-stitch chart?

Yes — GridGen overlays a counting grid where each square is one stitch. Use By size to fix the cell size, turn on labels to count rows and columns, and export the PDF. It does not pick thread colors for you; it gives you the countable grid to work from.

Does GridGen choose the thread colors or make a symbol chart?

No. It overlays the grid and row/column labels only. It does not reduce the image to a palette or assign symbols or color codes — you choose colors as you stitch.

How do I make one cell equal one stitch?

Use By size and set the cell size in pixels. Every square is then the same size across the image, so one cell maps to one stitch, bead, or pixel.

What craft formats does this work for?

Any counted craft that works from a grid — cross-stitch, needlepoint, pixel art, diamond painting, beadwork, perler/fuse beads, and latch hook.

Is it free and private?

Yes. GridGen is free and open source, and your photo is processed entirely in your browser, so it never leaves your device.
